The Problem: AI Assistants Are Flying Blind

Why your AI coding tool spends most of its time just exploring — and how one local tool changes everything

Your AI Guide Is Navigating Without a Map

Picture a brilliant tour guide — knows history, architecture, local secrets — but has never visited this city. Every question you ask sends them reading every street sign, peeking into every building, flipping through directories. Forty minutes later: you have directions.

That's what Claude Code, Cursor, or Codex does with your codebase. Every single task starts from scratch. The AI reads files, searches for patterns, and pieces together how things connect — every time you ask a question.

📊
The Real Cost

On a typical codebase task, AI coding tools make 40–50 tool calls just exploring before any real work begins. Most of that exploration is wasted — the same paths walked again and again, on every single query.

What If the Map Was Already Built?

CodeGraph indexes your entire project once and stores everything in a local SQLite database. Every function, every class, every method — and crucially, every connection between them: what calls what, what imports what, what extends what.

When your AI then asks "how does login work?", CodeGraph answers instantly: here's the function, here's who calls it, here's what it calls. No wandering required.

What This Looks Like in Practice

TypeScript (src/index.ts) // One-time setup const cg = await CodeGraph.init('/my-project'); await cg.indexAll(); // Instant answers forever after const callers = await cg.getCallers('AuthService.login'); const callees = await cg.getCallees('AuthService.login'); const ctx = await cg.buildContext('explain login flow');
